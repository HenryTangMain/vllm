# Systematic Flow

A design philosophy rooted in the visual language of process engineering diagrams and particle physics event displays — where time moves horizontally, parallel lanes reveal concurrent activity, and the density of marks tells the story of utilization.

## Philosophy

Space is time. The horizontal axis is sacred — an unbroken arrow from left to right, marking the passage of computation in discrete, measurable steps. Vertical space represents capacity: parallel lanes stacked with clinical precision, each one a channel through which work flows. The philosophy treats empty space not as rest but as waste — voids in a lane are inefficiencies made visible, accusations rendered in negative space. A fully utilized system fills its lanes with dense, rhythmic blocks; an idle system exposes pale, barren stretches that demand attention through their very emptiness.

Color is categorical, never decorative. Each hue belongs to a class of operation — one for initial computation, another for generation, a third for waiting. The palette is deliberately restrained: three or four colors maximum, chosen for maximal distinguishability at small scale. Blocks of color tile the lanes like specimens mounted on slides, their boundaries crisp, their interiors uniform. There is no gradient, no shadow, no dimension beyond the flat, honest rectangle. This is the aesthetic of the oscilloscope trace, the Gantt chart, the genome browser — tools built for experts who read density and pattern the way others read prose. Every element is placed with the meticulous precision of a master cartographer, the product of countless hours of spatial calibration.

Typography exists only as annotation — thin, monospaced labels that name the lanes, mark the phases, whisper measurements. Text never explains; it indexes. A label says "Req A" or "prefill" or "decode" and trusts the reader to understand through the visual pattern, not through description. The type is small, precise, and set in a technical face that evokes laboratory notebooks and engineering specifications. Painstaking attention to alignment ensures every label sits at exactly the right distance from its referent — close enough to associate, far enough to breathe.

Rhythm and repetition are the primary storytelling devices. A decode phase is a steady pulse of thin, evenly-spaced marks — the heartbeat of token-by-token generation. A prefill phase is a single dense block — a burst of concentrated work. Chunked prefill fragments that density into measured portions interleaved with the decode pulse, creating a visual syncopation that immediately communicates the concept of interleaving to anyone who sees it. The contrast between these rhythms — steady pulse versus burst versus interleaved pattern — is the entire argument made visible, requiring no words to comprehend. This expert-level visual orchestration reflects deep expertise in information design, where every spacing decision is the product of painstaking refinement.

The composition is structured as parallel scenarios — side by side or stacked — so the eye can compare directly. Alignment is absolute: time step 0 in one scenario sits directly above or beside time step 0 in another. This rigid registration allows differences to leap out through pattern recognition alone. The overall effect should feel like a page from an imaginary textbook on computational choreography — systematic, authoritative, and crafted with the obsessive care of someone who believes that how information looks is inseparable from what it means.
